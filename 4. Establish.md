# Chapter 4 - Establish: From New Birth to New Formation

If Chapter 3 asked how the church moves toward people, Chapter 4 asks what must happen after people respond. Engage brings people to the doorstep. Establish helps them build a life there.

This matters because conversion is a miracle, but it is not the end of the journey. A person may truly meet Christ in a moment, but that new life must still take root in thought, desire, habits, relationships, and daily obedience. Identity must become durable. A new believer must learn not only that he belongs to Christ, but how to remain, grow, repent, endure, and live under Christ's lordship.

## The Battle After Beginning

Nobody comes to Jesus in a neutral environment. New believers do not step out of repentance into an empty field. They step back into homes, screens, habits, wounds, ideologies, appetites, friendships, and fears that have already been forming them for years. Old patterns do not surrender politely. Rival stories do not stop speaking. Temptation does not disappear because someone has made a sincere beginning.

Jesus warned about this in the parable of the sower. Some hear the word and never receive it deeply. Some receive it with joy, but because they have no root, they fall away when pressure comes. Others begin well, but the cares of the world, the deceitfulness of riches, and competing desires choke the word until it becomes unfruitful. The point is not that the seed is weak. The point is that formation matters.

Engage scatters the seed. Establish helps the roots go down. Without rootedness, a person may have real sincerity and real initial movement toward Christ, yet still remain vulnerable to the same forces that shaped them before.

## How God Changes People

We cannot change ourselves at the deepest level. Willpower can modify behavior for a while. Fear can produce outward compliance. Religious pressure can create appearances. But only God can give new life, soften the heart, awaken love for holiness, and renew the mind.

Yet God does not usually form His people by bypassing their participation. He does not save people as machines. He forms sons and daughters. He teaches obedience. He trains affection. He renews the mind and reorders desire. His transformations are supernatural, but they are not impersonal.

That is why Romans 12 does not say, "Wait passively until change arrives." It says, "Be transformed by the renewal of your mind." God is the One who transforms, but He does so through means He has appointed. He changes people through truth received, sin confessed, prayer practiced, burdens shared, habits reordered, and obedience learned in real time.

The church's role, then, is not to pretend it can manufacture spiritual life. Its role is to steward the environment where life grows. Farmers do not create life, but they still till soil, water faithfully, and protect what has been planted. In the same way, the church cannot create holiness, but it is commanded to teach, pray, correct, encourage, carry, and walk with believers until the truth takes root.

## The Three Streams of Establish

Scripture shows God using three streams again and again to mature His people: the Word of God, the Holy Spirit, and fellowship. Remove any one of these, and growth becomes unstable.

The Word of God establishes believers by renewing the mind. A new believer cannot withstand a hostile age with borrowed phrases and secondhand convictions. He needs Scripture not as a religious accessory, but as living truth. The Word teaches, exposes, corrects, cleanses, and anchors. It names sin, clarifies hope, and gives shape to obedience.

The Holy Spirit establishes believers by making that truth living and personal. The Spirit convicts, comforts, teaches, strengthens, and produces fruit that no church program can manufacture. Establish is not behavior management with Christian language attached. It is Spirit-empowered formation. New believers must be taught not only how to read the Bible, but how to pray dependently, respond to conviction, and walk with God rather than merely think about Him.

Fellowship establishes believers by refusing isolation. Many people drift not because they lack information, but because they try to survive spiritually alone. God did not design Christian formation as private spirituality. He designed it as shared life. In Acts, believers devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching, the fellowship, the breaking of bread, and the prayers. Those anchors belong together.

This is why tables matter so much. Around tables people slow down, speak honestly, confess weakness, ask questions, and learn to carry one another. Fellowship, in the biblical sense, is not socializing with a Christian accent. It is shared life in Christ. It is belonging with accountability, tenderness with truth, grace with growth.

## The Pattern of Establish

The early church did not congratulate people for responding to the gospel and then leave them alone with a sermon archive. It brought them into a people. They were taught. They were prayed for. They were known. They were corrected. They were sustained.

That remains essential now. Large gatherings can proclaim truth. They can clarify and encourage. But formation requires proximity. People need spaces where confession is normal, prayer is practiced, and the "one another" commands of the New Testament become visible life instead of admired ideals. Bear one another's burdens. Confess your sins to one another and pray for one another. Forgive one another as God in Christ forgave you. Those commands require more than attendance.

That also means the church must guard against counterfeit fellowship. A group can become a performance just as easily as a stage can. If leaders pretend to be perfect, new believers learn to hide. If trust is weak, honesty dies. If correction comes without tenderness, bruised people withdraw. Establish must never feel like "Get your act together." The Messiah does not break a bruised reed or quench a faintly burning wick. His way is patient, truthful, gentle, and serious all at once.

## The Aim of Establish

The goal of Establish is not to produce religious people who know Bible facts and can speak Christian vocabulary. The goal is to see people rooted deeply enough in Christ that they begin to think differently, desire differently, repent honestly, endure suffering, resist temptation, love truth, and live with durable hope.

When Establish is happening well, people do not become perfect, but they become rooted. They know where to go when temptation rises. They learn the difference between conviction and condemnation. They begin to pray instead of merely panic. They confess instead of hide. They discover that obedience is not the enemy of freedom, but one of its first fruits.

And once people are rooted, the church must not keep them in spiritual infancy. Establish is not an endless waiting room. It prepares believers for useful, durable, Spirit-shaped life in the body of Christ and in the world.

Engage brings people to Jesus. Establish roots them in Jesus. The next movement asks how rooted believers are then released to serve, witness, and multiply without skipping the formation that makes such release trustworthy.
