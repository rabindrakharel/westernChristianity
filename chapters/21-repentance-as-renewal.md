# Chapter 21 Repentance as Renewal

Repentance is not self-hatred. It is truthful return. Churches that cannot confess cannot heal.

Corporate repentance names patterns, not just incidents: exclusion, neglect, favoritism, spiritual pride, performative leadership, and resistance to accountability.

Renewal begins when confession becomes communal language, not occasional emergency response.

**Humble practice:** Build liturgies of confession into regular worship, with pathways toward restitution and repair.

## Big Idea
Without practiced confession, churches normalize denial and blame-shifting.

## Canadian Reality Check
In many communities, this issue is shaped by urban diversity, suburban mobility, rural isolation, and generational transition. Churches that face this reality with humility and courage become more credible to neighbors and more formative for disciples.

## Ministry Experiments (Start Small)
- Introduce monthly corporate confession and restitution pathways.
- Train leaders to model repentance publicly and specifically.
- Create follow-up processes for relational repair.

## Leadership Pitfalls to Avoid
- Confession without changed behavior.
- Repentance language used to manage optics.

## Discussion Prompts
1. What patterns require corporate confession in our church?
2. How will we practice repair, not just apology?
3. What is one concrete change we will begin this month, and who owns it?

## Prayer
Merciful God, teach us truthful repentance that bears fruit.
