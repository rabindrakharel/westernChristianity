# Fix Taxonomy

Use this file when the cleanup task needs a sharper diagnosis before editing.

## 1. Meta-Reader Scaffolding

Problem:

- prose addresses the reader instead of carrying the reader
- sentences explain the intended effect instead of producing it

Common signals:

- "the reader has to..."
- "readers can understand..."
- "this makes him emotionally legible..."

Fix:

- remove the reader-reference
- replace it with direct scene-setting or direct argument

## 2. Book-Architecture Scaffolding

Problem:

- prose comments on where a piece sits in the manuscript instead of advancing the prose

Common signals:

- "belongs near Chapter 1"
- "in this sequence"
- "the next example takes us"

Fix:

- restate the conceptual relevance without mentioning the book's outline

## 3. Abstract System Language

Problem:

- paragraph uses accurate but distant summary words

Common signals:

- stratified
- performative
- relevant
- legible
- destabilized
- identity claims

Fix:

- convert to visible institutions, actions, and stakes
- replace labels with port, court, market, prison, temple, household, tax, rank, crowd, accusation

Note:

- this often gets mislabeled as "passive writing"
- the deeper issue is usually abstraction, not grammatical passivity

## 4. Weak Human Stakes

Problem:

- the world is described, but the cost to ordinary people is not

Fix:

- name what people feared losing:
  honor, livelihood, family peace, safety, status, belonging, bodily security

## 5. Heading Drift

Problem:

- heading comments on what the section is doing rather than carrying the section's burden

Fix:

- rename headings with a controlling idea, not an editorial label

## 6. Transition Drift

Problem:

- section changes direction abruptly or with workshop language

Fix:

- bridge using conceptual continuity:
  pressure -> decision
  decision -> meaning
  meaning -> next pressure

## 7. Weak Chapter-Opening Bridges

Problem:

- chapter starts by explaining its place in the book instead of opening the burden directly

Common signals:

- "If Chapter 1 asked..."
- "Chapter 3 asks..."
- "The first movement is..."

Fix:

- remove outline commentary
- open with the chapter's live burden, pressure, or claim
- preserve continuity with prior chapters without sounding like a table of contents

## 8. Comparative Lecture Openers

Problem:

- opening compares one figure or section to another instead of carrying its own weight

Common signals:

- "If X shows..., Y shows..."
- "Unlike the previous chapter..."

Fix:

- cut the comparison unless it is doing real argumentative work
- start with tension, burden, or scene directly

## 9. Mechanical Repetition

Problem:

- repeated sentence frames flatten the prose

Fix:

- keep some cadence
- vary sentence openings and structures
- collapse duplicates into one stronger sentence

## 10. Precision Softness

Problem:

- facts are broadly right but phrased loosely enough to weaken trust

Fix:

- tighten dates, titles, ranks, institutions, and disputed claims
- signal uncertainty explicitly where necessary
