# Western Christianity: A Six-Chapter Renewal Framework

This manuscript is structured as a six-chapter book moving from the search for meaning and identity in Christ to disciple-making, formation, multiplication, and servant leadership.

## Overall Goal

The goal of the book is to help Western churches speak to a searching, distracted, and spiritually weary culture with a coherent biblical pathway that is identity-centered, discipleship-centered, and practically actionable. The argument moves from identity to assignment, from assignment to outward witness, and from witness to a multiplying church culture.

## Chapter Summary

### 1) [Who Are You?](1-Who%20are%20you%3F.md)
**Focus:** Recovering identity, purpose, and mission in Christ.

- Names the ache for meaning beneath ordinary North American life.
- Grounds human identity in what God says rather than in self-construction.
- Argues that life without mission is meaningless and calls the church to recover its purpose in Christ.

### 2) [What Are You Called to Do?](2-What%20are%20you%20called%20to%20do.md)
**Focus:** Recovering disciple-making as the church's central assignment.

- Shows that identity in Christ leads to mission rather than passive religious activity.
- Confronts false assignments such as celebrity, institutional maintenance, and political outsourcing.
- Re-centers the church on Christ's command to make disciples.

### 3) [Engage: Bringing Light Into a Searching World](3-Engage.md)
**Focus:** Moving outward with gospel presence among people who are lonely, searching, and spiritually tired.

- Explains why the church must move toward urgent human need with explicit gospel clarity.
- Addresses fear, shame, and other barriers to witness.
- Frames Engage as the first doorway in a disciple-making culture.

### 4) [Establish: From New Birth to New Formation](4.%20Establish.md)
**Focus:** Rooting believers in Word, Spirit, and fellowship.

- Explains why conversion must be followed by durable formation.
- Uses Romans 12, the parable of the sower, and Acts to show how believers are stabilized.
- Defines Establish as the patient work of helping identity and obedience take root.

### 5) [Empower: From Formation to Fruitful Multiplication](5-Empower.md)
**Focus:** Equipping and commissioning believers for the work of ministry.

- Shows that rooted believers are meant to be released, not retained as spectators.
- Emphasizes participatory, relational church life that can actually train and send laborers.
- Frames multiplication as the normal fruit of a healthy church rather than a rare extra.

### 6) [Do You Want to Lead?](6-Do%20you%20want%20to%20lead%3F.md)
**Focus:** Recovering servant leadership for a multiplying church.

- Reframes leadership as shepherding rather than performance.
- Calls leaders to authenticity, repentance, prayer, accountability, and courage under the Word.
- Connects leadership directly to feeding people, protecting formation, and releasing others into mission.

## How the Chapters Work Together

1. **Chapter 1** asks who we are and why our lives must have purpose in Christ.
2. **Chapter 2** asks what that recovered identity calls us to do.
3. **Chapter 3** shows how that calling first moves outward through Engage.
4. **Chapter 4** shows how new believers are rooted through Establish.
5. **Chapter 5** shows how rooted believers are released through Empower.
6. **Chapter 6** asks what kind of leaders can shepherd and multiply a people without turning leadership into performance or control.

Together, the book argues for one coherent movement: **identity, assignment, engagement, formation, multiplication, and servant leadership.**
